# Deck Brief — 三喵聊俄國勝利日閱兵

> Sample deck (image-first mode). The original was a long Mandarin-language podcast transcript turned into an 8-slide lecture deck. This brief is the slim, in-tree teaching version — the raw transcript and generated images stay outside this repository.

## Source Summary
- Type: Mandarin-language podcast transcript (host commentary on Russia's Victory Day parade).
- Length: ~30 minutes of speech, ~6000 words after transcription.
- Reliability: opinionated commentary, not primary historical source.

## Intended Audience
Mandarin-speaking viewers interested in current affairs and historical analysis. Lecture-style consumption (long-form, single voice), not a sales pitch.

## Presentation Goal
A 6-8 minute analytical walkthrough that lets the audience answer:

- What is the Victory Day parade actually displaying?
- Why does Russia insist on May 9 over May 8?
- How is WWII memory used as a current political tool?

## Central Thesis
這場閱兵不只是紀念二戰，而是在反覆展示「誰有資格代表勝利與正義」；當下的烏克蘭戰爭正在快速透支這份道德遺產。

## Narrative Arc
Question → Evidence → Mechanism → Consequence

1. Open with the question (cover).
2. Whole talk in one slide (thesis).
3. Anchor on the concrete date dispute (May 8 vs May 9).
4. Show the moral complexity of Soviet WWII history.
5. Reveal how anti-fascism becomes a discourse tool.
6. Trace the ritual shift from October Revolution to Victory Day.
7. Bring the deck back to the present (Ukraine war as legitimacy depletion).
8. Closing.

## Section Outline (8 slides)
1. Cover.
2. Thesis (the whole talk in one slide).
3. May 8 vs May 9 — date dispute.
4. The two faces of Soviet WWII history.
5. Anti-fascism as a discourse tool.
6. From October Revolution to Victory Day — the ritual shift.
7. Ukraine war as legitimacy depletion.
8. Closing.

## Material to Compress or Exclude
Most of the host's biographical asides, audio-only filler, and quotes from secondary commentators were dropped. Only material that serves the central thesis was kept.

## Risks and Assumptions
- The audience is assumed to be familiar with the basic outline of the WWII Eastern Front; a primer slide is intentionally out of scope.
- The thesis is opinionated; the storyboard frames every claim as the host's argument rather than as neutral history.
- Several political claims would require fact-checking before public publication; the brief flags this rather than smoothing over it.
